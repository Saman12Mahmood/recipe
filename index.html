Dessert kitchen management

A Complete Journey Through 12 Topics

Presented by Saman Mahmood

Topic 1: Pate Sucree and Shortbread

Pate Sucree

Procedure: Sift 250 g all-purpose flour, 100 g icing sugar, 2 g salt into a bowl. Cut in 150 g cold, cubed unsalted butter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Mix 50 g egg yolks with 20 mL cold water, add to flour mixture, stir until dough forms. Wrap and chill for 1 hour. Roll out to 3 mm thickness, line tart molds, dock base. Blind bake at 350°F (175°C) for 15–20 minutes until golden.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered cutting butter into flour for a tender, flaky crust.
2. Learned blind baking to prevent shrinkage and ensure even cooking.

Shortbread

Procedure: Cream 200 g softened unsalted butter with 100 g granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Sift 250 g all-purpose flour, 50 g cornstarch, 2 g salt together. Gradually add dry ingredients to butter mixture, mixing until just combined. Press dough into a 9-inch square pan or roll and cut into shapes. Chill for 30 minutes. Bake at 325°F (160°C) for 20–25 minutes until lightly golden. Cool slightly before cutting.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Developed creaming skills for smooth shortbread texture.
2. Understood cornstarch’s role in melt-in-the-mouth consistency.

Topic 2: Pate a Choux

Choux Pastry

Procedure: Bring 250 mL water, 100 g unsalted butter, 2 g salt to a boil. Remove from heat, add 150 g all-purpose flour all at once, stir vigorously until dough forms. Return to medium heat, stir until dough pulls away from pan sides. Transfer to mixer, cool slightly, add 4 eggs (200 g) one at a time, mixing until smooth and glossy. Pipe into shapes (eclairs, profiteroles). Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20–25 minutes until golden and hollow.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered cooking choux dough for piping consistency.
2. Learned gradual egg incorporation for smooth pastry.

Pastry Cream Filling

Procedure: Heat 500 mL milk until simmering. Whisk 100 g granulated sugar, 4 egg yolks, 40 g cornstarch until smooth. Temper egg mixture with hot milk, whisking constantly. Return to saucepan, cook over medium heat, stirring until thickened. Remove from heat, stir in 5 mL vanilla extract, 50 g unsalted butter until smooth. Cover with plastic wrap to contact, chill.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Acquired expertise in tempering eggs for lump-free cream.
2. Understood cornstarch thickening for stable filling.

Topic 3: Sponge Cakes

Genoise Sponge

Procedure: Whisk 6 eggs and 180 g granulated sugar over a double boiler until warm (40°C/104°F). Whip on high speed until thick, pale, and ribbon-forming. Sift 150 g cake flour over mixture, gently fold in. Fold in 50 g melted unsalted butter, 5 mL vanilla extract. Pour into greased, lined 8-inch round pan. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 25–30 minutes until springy and golden.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered warm egg method for genoise volume.
2. Learned delicate folding to maintain aeration.

Joconde Sponge

Procedure: Mix 150 g almond flour, 150 g icing sugar, 4 eggs until smooth and thick. Whip 3 egg whites with 50 g granulated sugar to soft peaks. Fold meringue into almond mixture. Sift 50 g cake flour over mixture, fold gently. Fold in 30 g melted unsalted butter. Spread onto lined half-sheet tray, bake at 400°F (200°C) for 10–12 minutes until golden.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Developed skills in combining almond batter with meringue.
2. Understood almond flour’s role in flavor and structure.

Topic 4: Custards and Creams

Crème Brûlée

Procedure: Heat 500 mL 35% cream with 1/2 vanilla bean until simmering, remove from heat. Whisk 5 egg yolks, 100 g granulated sugar until pale. Temper cream into yolk mixture, whisking constantly. Strain mixture, pour into ramekins. Bake in water bath at 300°F (150°C) for 30–35 minutes until set but jiggly. Chill 4 hours. Sprinkle 50 g sugar on top, caramelize with torch.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered tempering for smooth custard base.
2. Learned sugar caramelization for crisp topping.

Lemon Curd

Procedure: Whisk 120 mL lemon juice, 150 g granulated sugar, 4 egg yolks, 2 whole eggs in a saucepan. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thickened (80°C/175°F). Remove from heat, stir in 100 g unsalted butter, 5 g lemon zest until smooth. Strain, chill, cover with plastic wrap to contact.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Developed precision in cooking curd to avoid curdling.
2. Understood butter’s role in glossy, rich texture.

Topic 5: Churned Frozen Desserts

Mango Sorbet

Procedure: Blend 90 g granulated sugar with 3 g pectin. Combine sugar/pectin mix, 180 mL water, 60 g glucose in a saucepan, whisk until combined. Bring to a full boil, boil 1 minute. Cool over ice bath. Whisk in 510 g mango puree, 15 g lemon juice. Chill well, freeze in ice cream machine.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Understood pectin’s role in stabilizing sorbet texture.
2. Mastered rapid cooling to maintain flavor before churning.

Vanilla Bean Ice Cream

Procedure: Heat 750 mL milk, 240 mL 35% cream, 1/2 vanilla bean, 300 g granulated sugar until sugar dissolves and mixture is hot. Temper into 150 g egg yolks, whisking constantly. Return to saucepan, cook over low heat, stirring until thickened and coats a spoon (do not boil). Strain, cool over ice bath. Chill thoroughly, freeze in ice cream machine.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Developed proficiency in crème anglaise for creamy ice cream.
2. Learned to extract vanilla bean flavor effectively.

Raspberry Gelato

Procedure: Heat 125 g granulated sugar, 100 mL milk, 90 mL 18% or 35% cream until sugar dissolves. Cool mixture. Prepare 438 g raspberry puree, strain seeds. Combine milk mixture with puree. Freeze in ice cream machine. Remove, stir in 50 g partially thawed frozen raspberries. Freeze in container.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Acquired skills in balancing dairy and fruit for dense gelato.
2. Understood timing of adding fruit for texture and appeal.

Ice Cream Cone Tuile

Procedure: Cream 60 g butter, 120 g sifted icing sugar until smooth. Gradually add 60 g egg whites, mixing fully. Sift 60 g cake flour over mixture, fold gently. Spread thinly on silicone mat using stencil for cone shapes. Bake at 350–375°F (175–190°C) for 5–7 minutes until golden. Shape into cones while hot, cool to harden.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered spreading batter for thin, crisp tuiles.
2. Learned to shape hot tuiles quickly for desired forms.

Topic 6: Still Frozen Desserts

Hazelnut Dacquoise

Procedure: Blend 360 g ground hazelnuts, 220 g icing sugar in food processor, sift. Whip 350 g egg whites with 90 g granulated sugar, 2 g salt to soft peaks. Fold in hazelnut mixture. Pipe into four 8-inch discs on lined baking sheet. Sprinkle 100 g chopped toasted hazelnuts. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 10 minutes until golden.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered folding for light, airy dacquoise texture.
2. Understood precise baking for pliable result.

Dark Chocolate Ganache Filling

Procedure: Bring 150 mL 35% cream to a boil. Remove from heat, add 200 g semisweet dark chocolate, stir until melted and smooth. Cool to spreadable consistency. Divide evenly to fill two 8-inch cakes as a layer.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Learned cream-to-chocolate ratio for spreadable ganache.
2. Developed skills in cooling ganache for layering.

Lotus Biscoff Pâte à Bombe Parfait

Procedure: Boil 210 g granulated sugar, 65 g glucose, 120 g water to 116°C (241°F). Whip 10 egg yolks, slowly add hot syrup, whip until doubled. Cool, mix in 325 g Lotus Biscoff spread, 12 g vanilla extract, 4 softened gelatin leaves with paddle. Fold in 1 L whipped 35% cream, 300 g chopped dark chocolate. Assemble in 8-inch molds with two hazelnut dacquoise layers, one ganache layer, top with parfait. Freeze.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Acquired expertise in pâte à bombe for aerated parfait.
2. Understood gelatin and cream for stabilizing frozen desserts.

Dark Chocolate Glaze

Procedure: Boil 120 g 35% cream, 180 g granulated sugar, 145 g water. Add 60 g cocoa powder, cook to 102°C (216°F), stirring. Cool to 50°C (122°F). Add 8 g softened gelatin sheets, stir until melted. Use at 35°C (95°F) to coat frozen cakes.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Learned cooking glaze to precise temperature for gloss.
2. Mastered gelatin incorporation for setting on frozen dessert.

Topic 7: Frozen Mousse and Soufflé

Candied Pineapple

Procedure: Cut 1 pineapple in half top-to-bottom. Remove pulp, reserve peel in freezer. Cube pulp. Boil 1000 g sugar, 1000 g water. Add pineapple cubes, simmer until tender and translucent. Remove from syrup, reserve for frozen nougat.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered simmering fruit for candied texture.
2. Understood preserving peel for presentation.

Pineapple Champagne Sherbet

Procedure: Puree 1000 g pineapple until smooth. Combine with 500 g sugar, 1000 g champagne, stir until sugar dissolves. Chill thoroughly. Freeze in ice cream machine to soft-serve consistency. Transfer to container, freeze until firm.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Learned balancing sugar and alcohol for smooth sherbet.
2. Developed skills in churning fruit-based desserts.

Almond Sponge

Procedure: Mix 100 g almond paste, 100 g eggs with paddle until smooth. Add 25 g cake flour, mix well. Incorporate 25 g melted unsalted butter. Whip 100 g egg whites until frothy, add 50 g sugar, whip to stiff peaks. Fold meringue into almond mixture. Spread evenly on half sheet tray. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 5 minutes until golden.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Acquired proficiency in folding meringue for airy sponge.
2. Understood almond paste’s role in flavor and structure.

Frozen Nougat

Procedure: Toast 180 g blanched almonds, 40 g pistachios at 330°F (165°C) for 15 minutes, chop coarsely. Cook 50 g sugar, 30 g water, 110 g honey to 123°C (253°F). Whip 120 g egg whites, slowly add syrup, whip until cool. Chop 60 g candied orange peel, 60 g cherries, 75 g candied pineapple. Fold in 500 mL whipped 35% cream, nuts, fruits, 3 g vanilla extract. Layer with almond sponge in molds, freeze, cut into 1.5" slices.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered hot sugar syrup for stable Italian meringue.
2. Developed skills in assembling layered frozen desserts.

Italian Meringue

Procedure: Boil 250 g sugar, 75 g water to 110°C (230°F). Whip 150 g fresh egg whites on high when syrup hits 110°C. At 118°C (244°F), pour syrup slowly into egg whites. Whip until cool. Use to decorate pineapple shells, torch to caramelize, reserve for nougat slices.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Learned precise sugar syrup temperature for glossy meringue.
2. Acquired expertise in piping and torching meringue.

Topic 8: Meringue, Japonaise & Dacquoise

Pavlova

Procedure: Whip 200 g egg whites to soft peaks. Gradually add 200 g fine sugar, whipping until no grittiness remains. Fold in 200 g sifted icing sugar. Pipe or spread meringue into shapes on parchment-lined sheets. Bake at 200°F (93°C) for 1.5 hours. Cool in oven 20 minutes to prevent cracking.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered whipping egg whites for stable pavlova meringue.
2. Understood low-temperature baking for crisp exterior.

Passionfruit Curd

Procedure: Combine 10 mL lemon juice, 75 g passionfruit puree, 60 g fine sugar in saucepan, bring to boil. Whisk 4 whole eggs, 2 egg yolks, 60 g sugar. Temper hot passionfruit mixture into eggs, whisking. Return to saucepan, cook over medium heat until thickened, stirring. Remove, stir in 170 g unsalted butter, cool. Refrigerate.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Learned tempering to prevent curdling in curd.
2. Developed skills in balancing acidity and sweetness.

Japonaise Base

Procedure: Whip 3 egg whites with 80 g granulated sugar to firm meringue. Combine 55 g ground almonds, 20 g bread flour, 80 g granulated sugar, fold into meringue. Pipe concentric 2-3" circles on flipped parchment paper. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 10–20 minutes until golden brown.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Acquired expertise in folding nut-flour into meringue.
2. Mastered precise piping for uniform japonaise discs.

Chocolate Buttercream

Procedure: Boil 400 g sugar, 200 g water to 118°C (244°F). Whip 4 whole eggs until frothy, doubled. Slowly pour hot syrup into eggs, whipping. Add 908 g thinly sliced butter incrementally, whip until incorporated. Add 140 g melted caramel or dark chocolate, whip until smooth.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Learned Italian meringue-based buttercream with hot syrup.
2. Understood emulsifying butter and chocolate for spreadable texture.

Topic 9: Cheesecake

Chocolate Cookie (Crust and Decorations)

Procedure: Cream 135 g unsalted butter, 245 g brown sugar, 3 g salt, 1 g vanilla extract until fluffy. Add 2 beaten eggs in two additions, mix well. Mix in 250 g melted dark chocolate couverture. Sift 1 g baking soda, 180 g cake flour, 30 g cocoa powder, fold into wet mixture. Pipe into 2-inch rounds on lined tray. Bake at 340°F (171°C) for 18–20 minutes.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered creaming for uniform cookie texture.
2. Learned piping dough for consistent shapes.

Chocolate Cookie Crust

Procedure: Finely crush 175 g chocolate cookies into crumbs. Mix with 100 g melted unsalted butter until combined. Press evenly into bottom of 6-inch cake pan or ring to form compact crust. Chill in refrigerator to set before adding cheesecake filling.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Understood crumb-to-butter ratio for firm crust.
2. Developed skills in pressing crust evenly.

Cheesecake

Procedure: Blend 500 g full-fat cream cheese, 225 g granulated sugar at low speed until smooth. Add 450 g unsweetened Greek yogurt, 2 whole eggs, 4 egg yolks, blend slowly until incorporated. Pour into 6-inch pan with chilled chocolate cookie crust. Bake at 300°F (149°C) for 45 minutes until set. Cool, chill.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Learned gentle mixing to prevent cracking.
2. Mastered low-temperature baking for smooth texture.

White Chocolate Ganache

Procedure: Heat 115 g 35% cream, 10 g glucose to boiling, remove from heat. Pour over 225 g chopped white chocolate couverture in a bowl, stir until melted and smooth. Optionally blend for finer consistency. Cool slightly, use as glaze over chilled cheesecake.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Acquired expertise in melting chocolate with cream for glossy ganache.
2. Understood glucose’s role in enhancing texture and shine.

Topic 10: Mousse Cake

Vanilla Sponge

Procedure: Combine 500 g Lentia vanilla cake mix, 375 g room-temperature whole eggs, 150 mL water in mixing bowl. Whisk on low for 1 minute to blend. Whip on third speed for 10–12 minutes until voluminous. Spread evenly on parchment-lined full-size sheet pan. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 8–12 minutes until golden and springy. Cool, cut into 6-inch discs.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered whipping batter for light sponge.
2. Learned spreading batter for uniform baking.

Blackcurrant Mousse

Procedure: Warm 225 g blackcurrant puree to 46°C (115°F). Boil 60 g granulated sugar, 30 mL water to 118°C (244°F), pour into whipping 30 g egg whites, whip until cool for Italian meringue. Soften 3 gelatin sheets in cold water, drain, add to puree, stir until dissolved. Fold puree into meringue, then fold in 195 mL softly whipped heavy cream. Layer over sponge in 6-inch molds, chill.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Acquired expertise in Italian meringue for stable mousse.
2. Understood gelatin’s role in setting smooth mousse.

Fruit Glacage (Blackcurrant)

Procedure: Soften 5 g gelatin in cold water. Heat 45 g sugar, 30 g water, 15 g glucose until dissolved, remove from heat. Stir in gelatin until melted. Add 75 g blackcurrant puree, mix well. Strain through cheesecloth. Cool to 35°C (95°F), pour over chilled mousse cakes. Top with fresh fruit.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Learned precise temperature control for glossy glacage.
2. Developed skills in straining for mirror-like glaze.

Isomalt Decorations

Procedure: Divide 400 g isomalt into two portions, add desired food coloring to each. Melt each portion separately in saucepan over medium heat until liquid, stirring occasionally. Pour or pipe into bubble molds or freeform shapes on silicone mat. Cool and harden at room temperature. Remove, use to decorate mousse cakes.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered melting isomalt for durable decorations.
2. Understood color and shape for visual appeal.

Topic 11: Mille Feuille & Gateau St. Honoré

Pastry Cream

Procedure: Bring 1200 g milk, 300 g 35% cream to scald. Whisk 300 g sugar, 140 g cornstarch, 6 eggs, 25 g vanilla paste until smooth. Temper egg mixture with hot milk mixture, whisking. Return to saucepan, cook over medium heat until thickened. Transfer to tray, cover with plastic wrap to contact, refrigerate.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered tempering for smooth pastry cream.
2. Understood cornstarch for thick, stable consistency.

Choux Paste

Procedure: Bring 130 g water, 130 g milk, pinch of salt, 120 g unsalted butter to boil. Add 130 g bread flour all at once, stir quickly over heat until dough forms a ball and pulls from pan sides. Transfer to mixer, cool slightly. Add 104 g eggs (approx. 2) one at a time, mixing until smooth and firm. Pipe for Gateau St. Honoré.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Learned cooking choux dough for piping consistency.
2. Developed skills in gradual egg incorporation.

Creme Chantilly

Procedure: Combine 250 g 35% whipping cream, 40 g icing sugar, 2 mL vanilla extract in chilled bowl. Whip until stiff peaks form, avoiding overwhipping. Transfer to piping bag with St. Honoré tip for assembly.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered whipping cream to stiff peaks for stability.
2. Understood sugar and vanilla balance for flavor.

Caramel

Procedure: Heat 200 g sugar, 60 g water gently until dissolved, then boil. Add 20 g corn syrup or glucose, cook to rich golden color. Plunge pan base into ice water to stop cooking. Dip filled choux puffs into caramel, allow to set.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Learned cooking caramel to correct color and consistency.
2. Understood corn syrup’s role in preventing crystallization.

Mille Feuille

Procedure: Dock 3 puff pastry sheets, bake at 400°F (200°C) until golden, cool. Trim edges for uniform size. Spread 700 g pastry cream over one sheet, top with second sheet, spread 700 g more cream, top with third sheet bottom-side-up, press gently. Freeze until set. Warm 500 g soft fondant, mix 80 g with 10 g dark chocolate, pipe lines, marble. Cut into 1.5"x4" pieces, decorate with berries.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Developed precision in layering for uniform structure.
2. Mastered glazing with fondant for professional finish.

Gateau St. Honoré

Procedure: Dock 1 puff pastry sheet, cut into six 4.25-inch circles. Pipe choux paste border on each circle, pipe remaining paste into 36 small puffs. Bake at 370°F (188°C) until golden, cool. Pipe pastry cream onto puff pastry circles’ bottoms. Fill choux puffs with pastry cream, dip tops in caramel, place around circles’ edges. Pipe creme chantilly in center with St. Honoré tip.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Acquired skills in combining puff pastry and choux.
2. Learned decorative chantilly piping.

Topic 12: Tiramisu

Lady Finger Sponge

Procedure: Sift 155 g bread flour, 35 g cornstarch. Whip 6 egg whites with 170 g granulated sugar to firm peaks. Whisk 6 egg yolks with 30 g sugar, 3 mL vanilla until light, add 3 mL more vanilla. Fold yolk mixture into whites with hand whisk. Fold in flour mixture gently. Pipe 2-inch ladyfingers on lined trays, sprinkle with sugar. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 7–8 minutes until golden.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered folding for light ladyfinger sponge.
2. Learned piping uniform ladyfingers for consistency.

Coffee Syrup

Procedure: Combine 130 g granulated sugar, 130 g water in saucepan, heat to just below boiling. Remove from heat, stir in 20 g instant coffee, 10 g Kahlua liqueur, 10 g Marsala. Cool completely before use.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Understood flavor balance in coffee syrup.
2. Developed skills in preparing stable soaking syrup.

Tiramisu Filling

Procedure: Soften 750 g mascarpone with spatula. Whip 6 egg whites with 300 g sugar to medium peaks. Heat 6 egg yolks with 300 g sugar over double boiler to 63°C (145°F), whip until thickened and pale. Gradually add mascarpone to yolks, mix until incorporated. Fold egg whites into mascarpone mixture by hand, whisk then spatula. Refrigerate if runny.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Acquired expertise in stable mascarpone filling.
2. Learned folding for light, creamy texture.

Meringue Batons

Procedure: Whip 100 g fresh egg whites until frothy. Gradually add 200 g sugar, whip to stiff peaks. Fold in 100 g sifted icing sugar. Pipe meringue into baton shapes on lined tray. Dust with 10 g cocoa powder. Bake at 240°F (115°C) for 40–60 minutes until dry and crisp.

Learning Outcomes:
1. Mastered hard meringue for crisp decorations.
2. Developed precision in piping and dusting.
